<p><p>Microarrays in agriculture serve as powerful tools for gene expression analysis, enabling the study of plant and crop genetics. Oligonucleotide DNA microarrays (oDNA) utilize short, specific DNA sequences to probe and analyze gene activity, providing insights into genetic variations and traits. Complementary DNA microarrays (cDNA) involve the reverse transcription of mRNA into cDNA, allowing for the examination of gene expression levels. Both types facilitate advancements in crop improvement, pest resistance, and stress tolerance, driving innovation in agricultural biotechnology.</p></p>
