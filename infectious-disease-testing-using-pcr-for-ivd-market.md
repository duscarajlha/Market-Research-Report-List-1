<p><p>Infectious disease testing using PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) for In Vitro Diagnostics (IVD) encompasses two primary market types: direct and indirect detection. Direct detection involves identifying the pathogen's genetic material in a sample, providing rapid and precise results for infections. Indirect detection, on the other hand, assesses the host's immune response to the pathogen by examining specific antibodies or biomarkers. Both approaches are critical in diagnosing infections, guiding treatment decisions, and monitoring public health trends effectively.</p></p>
