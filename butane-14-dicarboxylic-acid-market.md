<p><p>Butane-1,4-Dicarboxylic Acid is produced via various processes such as cyclohexane oxidation, cyclohexene oxidation, and phenol hydrogenation. Cyclohexane oxidation involves converting cyclohexane to its corresponding carboxylic acid through a series of reactions with oxygen. Cyclohexene oxidation similarly transforms cyclohexene into the dicarboxylic acid, often using catalysts. Phenol hydrogenation involves the reduction of phenol, creating intermediates that can be further processed to produce Butane-1,4-Dicarboxylic Acid. Each method influences the yield, purity, and commercial viability of the product.</p></p>
