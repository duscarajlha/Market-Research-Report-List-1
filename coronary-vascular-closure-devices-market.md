<p><p>Coronary vascular closure devices (VCDs) are designed to repair vascular access sites after cardiac procedures. The market is divided into two main types: invasive and non-invasive VCDs. Invasive coronary VCDs are used in procedures requiring puncture, such as angioplasty, to seal the access point effectively. Non-invasive coronary VCDs, on the other hand, are employed in less invasive procedures, minimizing patient trauma and recovery time. Both types aim to enhance safety and improve outcomes in cardiovascular interventions.</p></p>
